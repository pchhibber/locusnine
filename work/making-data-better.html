Make Data Better

The first step towards managing enterprise data is knowing what you have.

Key Goals Addressed

Connect to an enterprise that consists of wide data sources, facilitate their automated scanning, create and maintain an accurate inventory of logical data assets in the enterprise, facilitate risk assessment and trace data lineage flows.

Primary Users

Users of Enterprise Applications that require transparency in the data that lies within their system such as Information Architects, Data Stewards and Application Owners.Users of Enterprise Applications that require transparency in the data that lies within their system such as Information Architects, Data Stewards and Application Owners.

Problem Brief

Large firms have a lot of data, and can't manage what they don't know they have. A lot of their data that isn't organized can be catalogued into an inventory. The inventory should be accurate, based on real data and complete. Inventories can be of physical or logical assets - the latter is harder but more useful to catalogue. Such a catalogue or inventory is an organized asset that would make life easier for a large firm.

Solution

The core products of the MDB enterprise suite consist of Discover, Curate, Protect and Flow.

  • These 4 components come together to ensure transparency across your entire data landscape.
  • Discover is a data source connection and management tool that connects to the database on the customers end. This allows the next step Curate, to begin.

Curate enables users to organize their physical data inventory in terms of the business concepts they represent.

  • They can train the machine learning based system by providing it with examples, which it uses to identify and recommend other data that potentially represent the same concepts.
  • A human reviews some of these recommendations and provides feedback. This, in turn, improves the overall system performance, accuracy and coverage.

Protect enables risk assessment for privacy regulations and compliances such as GDPR to ensure your customers and sensitive data are all protected.

  • It also generates detailed reports for what data your enterprise holds on any individual, by collating information across enterprise wide data sources.

As data flows between integrated applications, it may change. This can have a cascading effect on other application databases.

  • For regulatory and other reasons like application portfolio rationalisation, it is imperative to understand the source, intermediary and destination applications for business concepts of interest.
  • Flow allows you to effectively map the true source of application data anywhere in the system, which other applications it has flowed to and how it has changed as it flowed.
